Thanks for expressing interest in the American Founding Festival (April 17-19, 2025, Washington County, Utah)

Earlier this week, I was able to make a visit to make a visit to Washington County to visit some of the possible venues for holding the event. As it turns out, this weekend is a very popular weekend to be in Washington County. The two parks with the largest trees are already committed to other events, one hosts a large cars show on our weekend (30,000+ visitors) and another has a farmer's market that cannot be moved. That's the bad news.

The good news is that the county commissioners are very interested in supporting this event. It happens that the Washington County Fair will run from April 11 through April 19th. The fair will have a strong America 250 theme and the commissioners want to add our event as part of their America 250 kickoff. We've reserved a beautiful new park called  Grandpa's Pond Park  that is just 2 miles from the County fairgrounds in Hurricane and we'll run a shuttle service between the fair and the park.

The downside of the venue is that there are no mature trees at this park. However, the weather in Hurricane is beautiful in April (average high of 80 degrees) and the large pond that is directly adjacent to the park will also help moderate the temperature.  We still have a few issues to work out with the parks department (e.g. overnight sleeping in the park) but the Mayor of Hurricane is on board with supporting our event, so I think we'll be able to get the needed exceptions.

So, in short, the park is not ideal (lack of mature trees) but with the county being so supportive and being a part of their America 250 kickoff are big benefits.  

I'll provide more information as it comes in and I'll be glad to discuss the venue with you if you like, just give me a call (801-372-1683).

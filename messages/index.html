Here are two documents of particular relevance to April, 1775.
  1. Articles of Association: This is the first enduring collaboration among the colonies. The colonies have associated to come to the assistance of Massachusetts in response to the crisis that began with the Boston Tea Party. The association is less than six months old at the time of the Battles of Lexington and Concord. Look for George Washington's Signature.
  2. Bloody Butchery: This is a broadside printing of news articles about about the Battles of Lexington and Concord, along with a list of killed and wounded from each town. It also a poem honoring the sacrifice of the fallen.
